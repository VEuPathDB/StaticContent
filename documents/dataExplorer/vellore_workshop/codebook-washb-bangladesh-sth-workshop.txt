WASHB Bangladesh STH Workshop Dataset — Codebook
Source: WASH Benefits Bangladesh trial (washb-bang-sth-public.csv) from https://osf.io/ks28a/overview. Data available on this page includes the analysis dataset used to produce figures and tables in Ercumen et al. PLOS NTDs 2019. 
Subset: 25 variables selected for workshop use
Observations: 10,011 (7,187 with STH outcome data; 2,824 lost to follow-up / no stool)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 1: RANDOMIZATION VARIABLES
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

dataid          Compound ID
                type: numeric
                unique values: 5,190

clusterid       Cluster ID (720 clusters)
                type: numeric
                unique values: 720

block           Randomization block (90 blocks, each containing all 7 treatment arms)
                type: numeric
                range: 1–90

tr              Randomized treatment assignment
                type: string (label)
                values:
                  Control         (n=2,518)
                  Water           (n=1,245)  — safe water supply (chlorinated)
                  Sanitation      (n=1,232)  — improved latrines + child feces mgmt
                  Handwashing     (n=1,237)  — handwashing stations + soap
                  WSH             (n=1,276)  — Water + Sanitation + Handwashing combined
                  Nutrition       (n=1,253)  — lipid-based nutrient supplement + counseling
                  Nutrition + WSH (n=1,250)  — combined nutrition and WSH

NOTES
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- The trial enrolled children aged ~1–5y at baseline (2011–2012) in rural Bangladesh.
- STH outcomes were measured ~3 years post-intervention (2014–2016) by Kato-Katz.
- The WSH interventions (water, sanitation, handwashing) showed no significant reduction
  in STH prevalence; nor did the nutrition or nutrition+WSH arms.
- Missing data for sex, agey, and STH outcomes (n=2,824) represent children lost to
  follow-up or without a valid stool sample.
- Baseline covariates (momage, momedu, wealth, etc.) were measured at enrollment (2011–2013).
