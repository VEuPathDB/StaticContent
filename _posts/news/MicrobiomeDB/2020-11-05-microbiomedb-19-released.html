We are pleased to announce the release of MicrobiomeDB 19

New data in this release

  • The 'Daily Baby' study from the University of Oslow, Norway. This study set out to provide a high-resolution view of the microbial colonization process of 12 human infants (7 girls and 5 boys), using fecal samples obtained on a near daily basis during the first year of life. In total, 2684 stool samples were analyzed with an average of 224 samples (range 116–267) per infant.
  • The Ecology of Cystic Fibrosis (Eco-CF) study from Columbia University Medical Center. This study set out characterize the microbiome in sputum, oral cavity and bronchoalveolar lavage (BAL) from pediatric cystic fibrosis patients.
  • St. Louis Children's NICU biome study. This study examined the assembly of the gut microbiota of premature infants residing in a tightly controlled environment of a neonatal intensive care unit (NICU) at St. Louis Children's Hospital(NICU). 922 samples were collected from 58 preterm infants.

New features in this release

  • There are no new features in this release.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When sample IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
