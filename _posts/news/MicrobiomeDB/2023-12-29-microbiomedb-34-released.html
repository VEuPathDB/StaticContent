We are pleased to announce the release of MicrobiomeDB 34

New data in this release

  • There are no new datasets in this release.

New features in this release

  • New differential abundance tool - Using Masslin2, uou can now carry out a formal statistial analysis of your data direclty in the browser to identify any taxon that is differentially abundance between any two groups. If you're working with metagenomic data, this new tool can also be used to identify differentially abundant pathways! Results in conveniently visualized as an interactive volcano plot, making it easy to focus on the most interesting features.

Upcoming events

  • The MicrobiomeDB team will holding a in-person workshop at UPenn on December 4, 2023, as part of the PennCHOP Microbiome Program. If your lab or institution is interested in an in-person or virtual workshop, don't hesitate to reach out via our 'Contact Us' link.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits.
