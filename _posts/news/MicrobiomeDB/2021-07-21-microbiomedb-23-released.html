We are pleased to announce the release of MicrobiomeDB 23

New data in this release

  • The Anopheles albimanus 16S microbiome study assessed the impact of pyrethroid exposure on the internal and cuticle microbiome. A. albimanus is one of the main insect vectors responsible for transmission of malaria in Central America, northern South America, and the Carribean.
  • The Baby Observational and Nutrition Study (BONUS) includes shotgun metagenomic data from over 1200 samples collected longitudinally from infants diagnosed with cystic fibrosis during newborn screening, or healthy controls. The goal of this study was to understand the extent to which the microbiome in infants with CF is linked to poor growth in the first year of life.

New features in this release

  • Our alpha diversity app now includes Pielou's Eveness metric as one of the available diversity metrics.
  • Our correlation app has been updated with UI/UX improvements.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary.
