New data in {{project}} 68

Single cell RNA Sequence

  • Single cell expression and chromatin access of the T. gondii lytic cycle - scRNA-Seq – Transcriptome throughout the lytic cell cycle established at the single cell level. Gubbles lab prepublication.

Single cell ATAC Sequence

  • Single cell expression and chromatin access of the T gondii lytic cycle - scATAC-Seq – Chromatin accessibility maps throughout the lytic cell cycle established at the single cell level. Gubbles lab prepublication.

Proteomics

  • Comparative proteomics of 4 SAG1 KO clones by iLFQ – Whole cell shotgut proteomics of Toxoplasma gondii wild type and 4 knock out clones of SAG1. Data were analyzed by iLFQ methods. Hänggeli et al. 2023.
  • Comparative proteomics of 4 SAG1 KO clones by iTop3 – Whole cell shotgut proteomics of wild type Toxoplasma gondii and 4 knock out clones of SAG1. Data were analyzed by iTop3 methods. Hänggeli et al. 2023.

New features in {{project}} 68

  • The Sequence Retrieval Tool offers more input options such as uploading from a text file or URL. New options for configuring or choosing the type of sequence returned are also available.
  • The Product Descriptions table on gene pages is now called All Product Descriptions and contains product descriptions from all relevant sources. The most commonly used product description also appears at the top of the gene page.
  • The Text Search 'Fields' parameter values have changed. Choose 'Product descriptions (all)' to search the All Product Descriptions table on gene pages. Choose 'Product descriptions' to search only the commonly used product descriptions that appear at the top of gene pages.
  • The Organisms parameter in the Identify Genes based on SNP Characteristics search has a new look. Choose your organism of interest to load the Set of Samples parameter with that organism's WGS isolates.
  • Compressed files in the Download Data Files tool are now in .gz format.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
