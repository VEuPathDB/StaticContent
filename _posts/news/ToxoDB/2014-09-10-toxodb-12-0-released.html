We are pleased to announce the release of ToxoDB 12.0

New data in this release:
  • Bradyzoite in vivo transcriptome, M4 strain aligned to ME49: PolyA+ selected RNA was extracted from M4 strain 21 days in vivo bradyzoites and used to construct a strand specific mRNA library (Buchholz et al.).  View experimental details and available searches in the dataset section.  
  • Toxoplasma gondii GT1 and RH tachyzoite transcriptomes time series: Transcript expression was assayed by in T. gondii RH and GT1 tachyzoites by Illumina sequencing at several time points post infection. This data was obtained with funding from the EuPathDB driving biological project mechanism and has been provided prepublication. View experimental details and available searches in the dataset section. 

  • Gregarina niphandrodes genome sequence and annotation: This data was generated as part of the Toxoplasma sequencing white paper project. View experimental details and available searches in the dataset section. 

  • Eimeria tenella sporozoites rhoptry proteome (Oakes et al.): Highly enriched preparations of rhoptry organelles from E. tenella (Wis) sporozoites were analyzed by 2DE, LC-MS/MS.  View experimental details and links to searches in the dataset section. 

  • Two new data types with searches have been added in this release: Compounds and Metabolic Pathways.  This beta-release of Compound and Metabolic Pathways data sets incorporates KEGG pathways and a subset of PubChem records. Future releases will include compounds for additional metabolites and small molecules, and pathways from other sources such as MPMP, MetaCyc, etc. Please explore the site and contact us with your feedback.

    • Metabolic Pathways may be searched based on pathway Name/ID, genes, or compounds.
    • Compounds may be searched based on compound ID, text (synonym, InChI, etc.), enzymes, metabolic pathway, molecular formula or molecular weight.



 New features:
  • Results analysis tools:  results of gene searches can now be analyzed for statistical enrichment of Gene Ontology (GO) terms, metabolic pathways or gene product text terms. A detailed description of this new functionality and a video tutorial are also available.
