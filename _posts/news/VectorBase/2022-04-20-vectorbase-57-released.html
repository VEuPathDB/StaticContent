New features in {{project}} 57

BLAST Tool Improvements

  • The BLAST (multi-query capable) tool, known previously as BLASTbeta, can perform queries with one or more FASTA-formatted sequences and offers many download types. The tool uses NCBI BLAST to find genes, genomic sequences, ESTs, or PopSet Sequences that have BLAST similarity to your input sequence. BLAST (multi-query capable) is available from the tools menu.
  • A new download option, 'This table (csv)', for the results of multi-sequence queries (combined result) retrieves the {{project}} result table for the results of multi-sequence queries (combined result).
  • View tutorial GIF.

Gene page sequence highlighting

  • Genomic and mRNA sequences on gene pages have a new color scheme. Introns are highlighted in yellow and UTR sequence are highlighted in pink. Previously they were gray and blue, respectively. Example

Community Annotations Updated

  • Anopheles gambiae PEST – Changes in the genome annotation that were entered into Apollo by {{project}} users were reviewed by VEuPathDB annotation staff and transferred from Apollo into the VEuPathDB annotation track.

    • RNA Sequence

    • Pyrethroid insecticide exposure (deltamethrin or alpha-cypermethrin) in filed mosquitoes from Guatemala and Peru, and a susceptible colony from Guatemala – Three biological replicates with pools of 5 mosquitoes each were prepared according to the following: the susceptible laboratory colony Sanarate (San); field-collected mosquitoes from Guatemala not exposed to insecticide (GTM-unx) and alive after exposure to deltamethrin (GTM-delta); field-collected mosquitoes from Peru not exposed to insecticide (PER-unx), alive after exposure to deltamethrin (PER-delta) and alive after exposure to alpha-cypermethrin (PER-acyp). Mackenzie-Impoinvil et al. 2019
    • Organophosphate and pyrethroid insecticide resistance – Total RNA was isolated from three pools containing five mosquitoes each from the following groups: mosquitoes phenotyped as resistant following a malathion or permethrin bioassay, non-insecticide exposed mosquitoes and susceptible An. arabiensis colony mosquitoes from Dongola and Sekoru strains. Messenger et al. 2021
    • Leishmania infantum-infected Lutzomyia longipalpis midguts – RNA-Seq of uninfected and Leishmania infantum-infected Lutzomyia longipalpis midguts from seven different libraries corresponding to time points which cover the various Leishmania developmental stages inside the vector. Coutinho-Abreu et al. 2020
    • Transcriptional response to blood (with and without parasites) at 1, 4, or 9 days post blood meal – Female sand flies aged 5 to 9 days were fed blood or parasite-containing blood, resulting in four treatment groups: (i) blood meal only, (ii) blood meal + L. major, (iii) blood meal + L. donovani and (iv) blood meal + H. muscarum. Flies were then left to develop an infection or digest their non-infected blood meal for 1, 4 or 9 days Sloan et al. 2021

Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
