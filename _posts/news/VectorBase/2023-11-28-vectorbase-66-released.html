New data in {{project}} 66

Genome Sequence and Annotation

The following organisms are represented in VectorBase for the first time.
  • Lutzomyia longipalpis M1 – A vector of Leishmania parasites that cause visceral leishmaniasis. A single male (M1) specimen of Lutzomyia longipalpis, derived from the Jacobina strain was sequenced using PacBio HiFi and Illumina HiC technologies. GCF_024334085.1; Labbé et al. 2023
  • Phlebotomus papatasi M1 – A vector of Leishmania parasites that cause cutaneous leishmaniasis. The genome of individual one male (M1) from the PPIS colony was sequenced by PacBio HiFi using the PacBio Ultralow input library prep, and additional material from the PPIS colony was used to generate HiC data for scaffolding the reads. GCF_024763615.1.
  • Anopheles marshallii AmarGA1 – This assembly is based on 51x PacBio HiFi data, 10X Genomics Chromium data, and Arima Hi-C data generated by the Anopheles Reference Genomes project. GCF_943734725.1.
  • Sabethes cyaneus ScyaPA1 – This assembly is based on 37x PacBio HiFi data and Arima Hi-C data generated by the Anopheles Reference Genomes project. GCF_943734655.1.
  • Anopheles nili AnilCM1 – This assembly is based on 48x PacBio HiFi data, 10X Genomics Chromium data, and Arima Hi-C data generated by the Anopheles Reference Genomes project. GCF_943737925.1.
  • Biomphalaria glabrata XG47 – This assembly is based on 29x PacBio data and Arima2 Hi-C data generated by the Wellcome Sanger Institute Tree of Life Programme. GCF_947242115.1.

Genome Reference and Strain Name Changes

In response to community input we maintain the ongoing transition to VectorBase new reference genomes. VectorBase reference genomes are chosen based on diverse metrics such as assembly level (chromosomes or scaffolds and their quantities), BUSCO, and N50, and in consideration of the designation of genomes as reference at NCBI. All functional data, including RNAseq, will be aligned to the updated reference genomes. While the previous assemblies will persist on VectorBase indefinitely, their functionality will be limited. The new assemblies will encompass aligned omics datasets and comprehensive site functionality.

A notification banner on the genome page of relevant species, will indicate these changes. If you have feedback or inquiries, require assistance, or encounter difficulty locating your genes of interest in the new genomes, please feel free to Contact Us.
  • Lutzomyia longipalpis M1 now serves as the VectorBase reference for Lutzomyia longipalpis strains. All functional genomics data will be aligned to this genome.
  • Anopheles darlingi AdarGF1 – The strain name was changed from idAnoDarlMG-H_01 to AdarGF1 beginning with VectorBase 66. This organism now serves as the VectorBase reference for Anopheles darlingi strains. All functional genomics data will be aligned to this genome.
  • Phlebotomus papatasi M1 now serves as the VectorBase reference for Phlebotomus species. All functional genomics data will be aligned to this genome.

Annotation Updates

Culex quinquefasciatus JHB 2020 – The genome annotation for Culex quinquefasciatus JHB 2020 has undergone significant revisions. VectorBase users contributed changes through Apollo, which were subsequently reviewed by the VEuPathDB annotation staff and integrated into a new gene set, with the structural annotation version transitioning from Dec 04, 2020, to Jul 17, 2023.

A total of 425 transcripts corresponding to 391 genes were updated, including both structural (intron/exon boundaries) and functional (name/symbol and product description/putative function) annotations. The alterations encompass the creation of new genes, modifications to existing ones, deletions, merges, splits, and rectifications to ensure accurate gene annotation.

Attributions for these modifications go to Theresa Nicole Menna (163 Ionotropic and 53 gustatory receptors), Rong Guo (28 odorant binding proteins) from the Megan Fritz lab at the University of Maryland, and Vitor L. Dos Anjos (181 Odorant receptors) from the Carolyn (Lindy) McBride lab at Princeton University. The distribution of these changes is shown in per event type.
