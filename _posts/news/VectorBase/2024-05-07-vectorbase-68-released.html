New features in {{project}} 68

  • Long gene product descriptions are now trucated in relation to the width of your browser window. The full product description is available on mouseover and individual transcript product descriptions are available in the All Product Descriptions table on gene pages. Truncated descriptions GIF.
  • The Orthologs and Paralogs table on {{project}} gene pages also serves as a Clustal Omega tool for retrieving alignments between orthologs. This table now includes 'Selection Count' and 'Selection Clear' options for improved tool function. Clustal Omega Counts GIF.
  • The Send To tool is now offered in VEuPathDB.org Strategies. Results created in VEuPathDB.org Strategies can now be ported to {{project}} My Strategies for further investigation. Only genes present in {{project}} species will be included in the ported strategy results. VEuPathDB.org Send To GIF.
  • Newly sorted strategy result tables will now default to page 1 – Search result tables can be sorted based on any column of data. When a table is sorted while viewing page 3, users are returned to page 1 to view the sorted data table.

New data in {{project}} 68

Species Complex

Anopheles coustani AcouGA1 and Anopheles ziemanni AzieGA1 genomes are part of a species complex. Based on the current evidence, An. ziemanni AzieGA1 may actually be the species An. coustani or even another species from this complex. We have banners on each gene page for both of these genomes. Genome Reference Changes

When genomes from multiple strains of an organism are integrated into {{project}}, one strain is chosen to serve as the {{project}} reference to which all functional genomics data are aligned. The following reference changes were made in Release 68.

Anopheles sinensis China
  • Sialotranscriptome: Female adult fed 10% glucose for salivary gland dissection – Female adult mosquitoes were fed with 10% glucose solution and collected at 3-5 days post-emergence for salivary gland dissection. A total of 180 salivary glands were collected (one biological sample). Fang et al. 2018.
  • Male and female adults from a resistant strain – Male and female adults from a resistant strain (one biological replicate). PRJNA339810.
  • Pooled male and female 30 days old – Pooled male and female 30 days old, resistant vs susceptible (one biological replicate). PRJNA293400.
  • Whole body from a 2 - 3 day old female mosquito – Mosquitoes were reared under insectary in screened cages at 28 ± 2 °C, 70-75% humidity. Adult mosquitoes were fed with glucose solution and water-soaked sponges. After eclosion, adult female samples were pooled (one biological sample). Feng et al. 2018.
  • Pooled eggs, larvae, pupae, male and female adults – Ten percent, 30%, 20%, 20%, and 20% of total RNA quantity separately from eggs, larvae, pupae, male adults, and female adults RNA samples were pooled together for cDNA preparation (one biological sample). Chen et al. 2014.
  • Antenna transcriptome pre- and post- blood meal – Transcriptome analysis of the antennae of Anopheles sinensis which were kept either on sugar diet or five hours after blood feeding (three biological replicates each treatment). Chen et al. 2017.
  • Pooled male and female larvae, pupae, and adults – Produced from pooled male and female larvae, pupae, and adults (three An. sinensis samples). Neafsey et al. 2015.

Biomphalaria glabrata XG47
  • Long-term Schistosoma mansoni infection – Snails developing schistosome parasite during long term (3,5,8 week) infection (one biological sample). PRJNA285250.
  • Embryonic and larval stages mixed – Early cleavage stage embryos up through to advanced veliger larval stages until the point before hatching (one biological sample). Kenny et al. 2016.
  • Adult tentacles – Adult tentacles (one biological sample). PRJNA282347.
  • Inbred lines homozygous for three PTC2 alleles (R, S1, and S2) impacting susceptibility (S) and resistance (R) to S. mansoni PR-1 – When exposed to Schistosoma mansoni PR-1, parasite resistance in Biomphalaria glabrata 13–16-R1 depends on genotype at the PTC2 genomic region, as determined by a resistance allele (R) and two susceptibility alleles (S1 and S2). RNA-Seq data were generated from three inbred lines homozygous for these alleles: i68 (R), i172 (S1), and i4 (S2) (one biological sample for each treatment). Tennessen et al 2020.
  • Twelve (12) different tissues/organs from adults – Twelve different tissues/organs were dissected from several individual adult BB02 B. glabrata snails (one biological replicate for each). Adema et al. 2017.
  • Treatments (stranded RNASeq): Response to S. mansoni infection in resistance-selected snails – B. glabrata and S. mansoni were collected on the island of Guadeloupe, West Indies. We selected for resistance by challenging snails with S. mansoni miracidia. Fine-mapping and RNA-Seq characterization reveal a region, the Guadeloupe Resistance Complex (GRC). We perform RNAseq on challenged and unchallenged reesistance-seleceted snails of various GRC genotypes. Tennessen et al. 2015.
  • Controls (unstranded RNASeq): Response to S. mansoni infection in resistance-selected snails – B. glabrata and S. mansoni were collected on the island of Guadeloupe, West Indies. We selected for resistance by challenging snails with S. mansoni miracidia. Fine-mapping and RNA-Seq characterization reveal a region, the Guadeloupe Resistance Complex (GRC). We perform RNAseq on challenged and unchallenged reesistance-seleceted snails of various GRC genotypes. Tennessen et al. 2015.

Population Biology

All the datasets listed below are available in the context of 700+ other studies in the Interactive MapVEu Tool (beta).
  • Biting behavior of malaria vectors in remote communities of the Loreto region – Morales Viteri et al. 2021, VBP0000852 on MapVEu.
  • Dispersion of malaria vectors in a community near the city of Iquitos 2021 – VBP0000856 on MapVEu.
  • Fine-scale spatial distribution of deltamethrin resistance and population structure of Anopheles funestus and Anopheles arabiensis populations in Southern Mozambique – Mosquitoes were collected from 11 towns in southern Mozambique, as well as from different neighbourhoods within the town of Palmeira, during the peak malaria transmission season in 2016. CDC bottle bioassay data is available in this study. Das et al. 2023, VBP0000850 on MapVEu.
  • Malaria vectors and their behavior during the rising and falling seasons in the community of Santa Emilia – Collections were carried out during the rising season in April and the falling season in October. Three nights of collection were conducted using the HLC method to observe intra-peri bite patterns during a 12-hour collection period. VBP0000854 on MapVEu.
  • Molecular and entomological surveillance of malaria vectors in urban and rural communities of Benguela Province, Angola – Adult mosquitoes were collected indoors using CDC light traps from nine randomly selected households at two sentinel sites with distinct ecological characteristics. The samples were collected from 1 February to 30 June 2022. Pathogen data, looking at the presence of P. falciparum and blood meal source data is available for this study in VectorBase Alves et al. 2024, VBP0000851 on MapVEu.
  • Mosquito Surveillance For St. Joseph County, IN 2023 – VBP0000858 on MapVEu.



Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
