New features in {{project}} 55

  • My Organism Preferences is a new option for filtering menus to a subset of organisms in {{project}}. Use the My Organism Preferences page to choose the organisms you are interested in. {{project}} will function as if the database contained only your chosen organisms. The feature is easily disabled/enabled at any time from a button located under the header on every page. More Details
  • The Reference sequence track in JBrowse and PBrowse has a new color scheme. Blah Blah
  • Galaxy Version updated on 8 Oct, 2021: This updated version of the Galaxy software improves job and workflow performance. In addition to the previous set of tools and workflows, we now offer additional tools, such as those for scRNA-seq (Scanpy, Seurat), species ID (CryptoGenotyper), table manipulation (Datamash), and proteomics (MSstats, Search GUI, Peptide Shaker, MaxQuant). Certain tools have been upgraded and now function without errors: bamCoverage, bgzip, tabix and Upload File.

New data in {{project}} 55

Genome Sequence and Annotation

  • Anopheles merus MAF 2021 – PRJNA660041.
  • Aedes albopictus Foshan FPA – This organism is updated with stable IDs of format AALFPA_* (AALFPA_058096). Old IDs are mapped to new, so searching with old IDs will return the pages labeled with the new ID. Transcripts use the gene stable id + ".R" + a number (AALFPA_058096.R24198). Proteins use the same id as their transcript, substituting ".P" for the ".R" (AALFPA_058096.P24198).

RNA Sequence

Anopheles coluzzii Ngousso
  • Male mosquito contribution towards malaria transmission: mating influences the Anopheles female midgut transcriptome – Female mosquitoes were blood or sugar feed. In both groups, some were kept with males for mating (virgins were in separate cages). Injections were done with ecdysone plus ethanol as the carrier, or ethanol alone, no injection controls were also used. Dahalan et al. 2019.
Anopheles culicifacies A-37
  • Transcriptome of olfactory tissues (antennae, maxillary palp, proboscis and labium), dissected at three time points with different types of meals – Female mosquito An. culicifacies A. Olfactory tissues combined to make a single pool for each time point: 1-2 day old naive sugar feed, 5-6 day old 30 min blood feed, 5-6 day old 30 hrs blood feed. Das De et al. 2018.
  • Transcriptome of the brain, dissected at three time points with different types of meals – Total RNA was extracted from the collected brain tissues (approximately 30 mosquitoes were pooled to form one single sample): naïve, 30min, and 30h post blood meal (PBM). Das De et al. 2018.
Glossina brevipalpis IAEA
  • Mid-gut and bacteriome transcriptomes from low vector competence tsetse flies – Mid-gut and bacteriome RNA-Seq transcriptomes of teneral (1 day old, unfed) virgin tsetse flies from Glossina brevipalpis (low vector competence). Medina Munoz et al. 2021
Glossina morsitans YALE
  • Mid-gut and bacteriome transcriptomes from high vector competence tsetse flies – Mid-gut and bacteriome RNA-Seq transcriptomes of teneral (1 day old, unfed) virgin tsetse flies from Glossina morsitans (high vector competence). Medina Munoz et al. 2021

Proteomics

Anopheles gambiae PEST
  • Brain Proteomics – A large-scale mapping of the brain proteome of An. gambiae was carried out using high-resolution tandem mass spectrometry. Dwivedi et al. 2014
  • Proteome of head samples – 1) whole head including all appendages (maxillary palps, proboscises and antennae), 2) only antenna, 3) only eyesChampion et al. 2015
  • Pupal cuticles and larval head capsules – Proteomics analysis of the cast head capsules left behind when fourth instar larvae molted to pupae and on cast whole body cuticles left behind when pupae molted to adults.He et al. 2007
Ixodes scapularis Wikel
  • Changes in the Proteome of Langat-Infected Ixodes scapularis ISE6 Cells – High resolution LC-MS/MS was used to analyze the proteome of Langat-infected ISE6 cells Grabowski et al. 2016

Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
