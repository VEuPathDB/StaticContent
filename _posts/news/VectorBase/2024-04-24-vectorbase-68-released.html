New features in {{project}} 68

  • The Sequence Retrieval Tool offers more input options such as uploading from a text file or URL. New options for configuring or choosing the type of sequence returned are also available.
  • The Product Descriptions table on gene pages is now called All Product Descriptions and contains product descriptions from all relevant sources. The most commonly used product description also appears at the top of the gene page.
  • The Text Search 'Fields' parameter values have changed. Choose 'Product descriptions (all)' to search the All Product Descriptions table on gene pages. Choose 'Product descriptions' to search only the commonly used product descriptions that appear at the top of gene pages.
  • Compressed files in the Download Data Files tool are now in .gz format.

Anopheles arabiensis DONGOLA 2021
  • Evolution of sex-biased gene expression aaraDongola – Male and female sex-tissues vs carcass transcriptomes. 3-4 day old adult males and 48 hour blood-fed females.   • Evolution of gene expression levels in Anopheles male reproductive organs aaraDongola – Gene expression was determined for Male accessory gland and testes. Izquierdo et al. 2019.
  • A. arabiensis RNA seq Raw sequence reads – Cross-species Y chromosome function between malaria vectors of the Anopheles gambiae species complex. Bernardini et al. 2017.
Anopheles coluzzii AcolN3

Phlebotomus papatasi M1
  • Wild-caught P. kandelakii female salivary glands aligned to P. papatasi – Female salivary glands from wild-caught Phlebotomus kandelakii from Tbilisi, Georgia. Oliveira et al. 2020.
  • Blood (with and without parasites) at 1, 4, or 9 days post blood meal – Female sand flies aged 5 to 9 days were fed blood or parasite-containing blood, resulting in four treatment groups: (i) blood meal only, (ii) blood meal + L. major, (iii) blood meal + L. donovani and (iv) blood meal + H. muscarum. Flies were then left to develop an infection or digest their non-infected blood meal for 1, 4 or 9 days (three biological replicates). Sloan et al. 2021.

Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it.
