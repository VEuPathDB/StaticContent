New data in FungiDB 54

Genome Sequence and Annotation: updated genomes

  • Trichoderma reesei QM6a 2017 – Wan-Chen Li et al. 2017.

Whole genome sequence of isolates for SNP analysis:

  • Blastomyces percursus
    • SNP calls on WGS isolates causing blastomycosis in Africa and the Middle East – Sequencing of Blastomyces percursus isolates causing blastomycosis in Africa and the Middle East. Schwartz et al. 2020.
  • Cryptococcus neoformans
    • DAR1401 isolate was added to the following dataset: SNPs calls on WGS of clinical Cryptococcus neoformans isolates recovered from patients undergoing anti-fungal treatment – Single-end reads of serial isolates from cryptococcal meningitis cases. Stone et al. 2019.
    • SNP calls on WGS of Cryptococcus isolates behind the outbreak in Queen Elizabeth University Hospital, Glasgow, Scotland, 2018 – Genome sequencing of Cryptococcus neoformans isolates. Farrer et al. 2021.

Transcriptomics

  • Fusarium fujikuroi
    • In planta expression profiles of Fusarium fujikuroi — Transcriptome of the fungi in infected maize roots growing in CM agar. Niehaus et al. 2016.
  • Coccidioides immitis
    • Transcriptional analysis of Coccidioides immitis mycelia and spherules — Analysis of gene expression changes in mycelia and young and mature spherules. Carlin et al. 2021.
    • Comparison of environmental (mycelia) to parasitic (spherule 96 hr) in the strains delta cts2/ard1/cts3 — An attenuated mutant strain C. posadasii delta cts2/ard1/cts3 in which chitinase genes 2 and 3 were deleted was previously created for vaccine development and profiled by RNA-Seq at two condition. Mead et al. 2020.
  • Mucor circinelloides
    • Transcriptomics of Mucor circinelloides NCRIP deficient spores phagocytosed by mouse macrophages — Transcriptomics of R3B2 and RDRP1 fungal spores and fungal spore-macrophage coculture. This data set was re-aligned against the genome of Mucor lusitanicus CBS 277.49 and now also contains the control sample L15 R7B 5h. Pérez-Arques et al. 2020.
  • Neurospora crassa
    • Comparative transcriptome of Neurospora crassa WT and delta mus52 strains — RNA-pool of both species extracted at 0min (control), 15, 30, 60, 120 and 240 min. Martins et al. 2018.
    • Neurospora crassa gene co-expression network — The co-expression network was used to determine the transcriptional response of Neurospora crassa to various carbohydrates. Niehaus et al. 2016.

Phenotype data

  • Manually curated Phenotypes (VEuPathDB) – We have merged the four different manually curated phenotype datasets that were previously available in FungiDB into a single combined dataset. This combined dataset hosts phenotypes from Aspergillus species (A. fumigatus Af293, A. nidulans FGSC A4, A. niger CBS 513.88 and A. oryzae RIB40), Cryptococcus species (C. neoformans JEC21 and H99 and C. gattii WM276), Fusarium graminearum PH-1 and Pyricularia oryzae 70-15. These phenotypes are manually curated from published literature describing phenotypes obtained from mutant studies. All these phenotypic annotations were previously available in FungiDB release 54 and no new data has been added in this release.

New features in {{project}} 54

  • OrthoMCL DB now shares web architecture with other VEuPathDB sites. The updated and streamlined interface emphasizes easy access to tools and help information.
    • OrthoMCL now includes orthology information for 564 species, including all of the VEuPathDB organisms with annotated genomes.
    • The redesigned home page includes a new site-wide search for easily finding groups, proteins or documents. A categorized list of Searches appears on the left of the home page and the center section is devoted to tutorials for getting you started with OrthoMCL features.
    • Group and protein record pages are redesigned and include a contents section for navigating to the data you are interested in.
    • For more information see the OrthoMCL DB About page, the FAQ page or complete this tutorial.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
