New data in FungiDB 46

Genome Sequence and Annotation: these annotated genomes appear for the first time in FungiDB.

  • Candida haemulonis B11899 – Muñoz et al. 2014.
  • Cyphellophora europaea CBS 101466 – Moreno et al. 2017.
  • Malassezia restricta KCTC 27527 – Park et al. 2017.
  • Mucor circinelloides f. circinelloides 1006PhL – Lee et al. 2014.
  • Phytophthora palmivora var. palmivora sbr112.9 – Ali et al. 2017.
  • Rhizophagus irregularis A1 (DAOM-664342) – Ropas et al. 2016.

Transcriptomics

  • Trichoderma reesei: Transcriptomic response to the two industrial feedstocks, corn stover and soybean hulls – mRNA samples were collected from WT, delta xyr1, delta ara1, and delta xki1 strains at 4 , 24 and 48 hr. Benocci et al. 2019.
  • Paracoccidioides lutzii: Transcriptional profile of cells exposed to argentilactone – Investigation of the antifungal potential of argentilactone via RNA-Seq of fungal cells. Araújo et al. 2014.
  • Aspergillus oryzae: Comparative transcriptomics of dormant and germinating conidia – Examination of gene expression in two developmental stages. Hagiwara et al. 2016.
  • Aspergillus niger: Comparative transcriptomics of dormant and germinating conidia – Examination of gene expression in two developmental stages. Hagiwara et al. 2016.
  • Aspergillus fumigatus: Comparative transcriptomics of dormant and germinating conidia – Examination of gene expression in two developmental stages. Hagiwara et al. 2016.
  • Cryptotoccus neoformans: RNA sequencing of cells treated with eltrombopag – Eltrombopag was investigated for suitability as an anticryptococcal agent. Ko et al. 2019.

Whole genome sequence of isolates for SNP analysis:

  • Aspergillus fumigatus: SNP calls on strains isolated from patients with PA and CNPA – WGS analyses of 17 strains isolated from patients in Japan with pulmonary aspergilloma and chronic necrotizing pulmonary aspergillosis. Takahashi-Nakaguchi et al. 2015.
  • Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis: SNP calls on Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis WGS (Hammersmith) – Isolates from various continents were sequenced using Illumina HiSeq 2000 technology. Farrer et al. 2013.
  • Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis: SNP calls on Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis WGS (BGI) – Isolates from various continents were sequenced using Illumina HiSeq 2000 technology.

Proteomics

  • Neurospora crassa: Circadian proteomics time course in wild type and delta csp-1 – Protein levels were accessed every 2 hr over 48hr time course and analyzed using tandem mass tag mass spectrometry (TMT-MS). Hurley et al. 2018.

Phenotype data

  • Manually Curated Fusarium Phenotypes – The phenotypes for Fusarium graminearum PH-1 are manually curated from published literature describing phenotypes obtained from mutant studies. We have curated and added phenotypes from Shin et al. 2017 publication on cytochrome P450 monooxygenases to the currently existing Fusarium phenotypes. We followed an "entity + quality" approach and curated phenotypes with multiple ontologies: Phenotypic Quality Ontology (PATO) for "quality" terms and Gene Ontology (GO), Fungal Gross Anatomy Ontology (FAO), Chemical Entities of Biological Interest (ChEBI) and Ascomycete Phenotype Ontology (APO) for "entity" terms.

  • PHI-base curated phenotypes – PHI-base is a web-accessible database that catalogues experimentally verified pathogenicity, virulence and effector genes from fungal, Oomycete and bacterial pathogens, which infect animal, plant, fungal and insect hosts. The pathogen-host interactions (PHI) phenotypes and pathogen-specific phenotypes from PHI-base were integrated for 36 different genomes available in FungiDB in this release. This dataset is current with PHI-base version 4.8. We have integrated 3,438 phenotypic annotations mapping to 2,089 genes in total. The original data file from PHI-base can be found in their GitHub site linked here. In addition, we have also updated the links to the PHI-base database in the linkouts section of gene pages. There are 2,307 PHI-base entries mapping to 2,089 genes in the current release.

New features in FungiDB 46

  • New Searches: Two new searches that return genes based on copy number data are available in the Genetic Variation section of FungiDB 46. The Copy Number search returns genes with a copy number in a resequenced strain that is within a range that you specify. The Copy Number Comparison search returns genes by comparing the gene copy number in one or more resequenced strains to its copy number in the reference genome.
  • Our pipeline of genomic analyses includes open reading frames (ORFs) prediction in addition to loading gene models from the official annotation. Because data usage analysis showed low ORF usage, in our next release (47) predicted ORFs will be available as a genome-scale fasta file from our downloads section and removed from the searches and genome browser.
