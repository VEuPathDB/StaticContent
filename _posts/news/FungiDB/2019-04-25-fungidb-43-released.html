New data in FungiDB 43

Genome Sequence and Annotation: these annotated genomes appear for the first time in FungiDB.

  • Aspergillus niger ATCC 13496 – Vesth et al. 2018.
  • Candida tropicalis MYA-3404 – Butler et al. 2009.
  • Cladophialophora carrionii KSF
  • Exophiala oligosperma CBS 72588 – Teixeira et al. 2017.
  • Fonsecaea pedrosoi CBS 271.37 – Moreno et al. 2017.
  • Kwoniella heveanensis CBS 569
  • Puccinia triticina 1-1 BBBD Race 1 – Cuomo et al. 2017.

New Genome Sequence: available for the first time in FungiDB:

  • Amauroascus mutatus UAMH 3576 – Whiston et al. 2015.
  • Naganishia albida JCM2334 – Vajpeyi et al. 2016.

Transcriptomics

  • Transcriptome of C. tropicalis cells under in vitro biofilm-forming conditions – Transcriptional profiling of a-type wor1 deleted cells (wor1-delta white) and mixed a-type and alpha-type opaque cells (mixed opaque) under in-vitro biofilm-forming conditions. Jones et al. 2014.
  • A. fumigatus responses under oxidative and iron stresses – RNA-Seq experiments examining A. fumigatus responses to oxidative stress and iron starvation. Kurucz et al. 2018.
  • Transcriptomic profiling of Cryptococcus cells in response to oxidative stress. – WT and gsb1 mutant were grown in YPD and in the presence of oxidative stress (H2O2) to identify transcriptional change s occurring in response to stress. Cheon et al. 2017.

Phenotype data

  • Manually curated Cryptococcus Phenotypes – The phenotypes for Cryptococcus neoformans JEC21 and H99 and Cryptococcus gatii WM276 are manually curated from published literature describing phenotypes obtained from mutant studies. We have curated and added phenotypes from Jung et al. publication on transcription factors to the currently existing phenotypic dataset for C. neoformans H99. We followed an "entity + quality" approach and curated phenotypes with multiple ontologies: Phenotypic Quality Ontology (PATO) for "quality" terms and Gene Ontology (GO), Fungal Gross Anatomy Ontology (FAO), Chemical Entities of Biological Interest (ChEBI) and Ascomycete Phenotype Ontology (APO) for "entity" terms.

Annotations from Gene Ontology Consortium (GOC) resources:

Curated and computed gene ontology (GO) annotations from Gene Ontology Consortium (GOC) resources have been integrated into FungiDB in Release 43 for Saccharomyces cerevisiae, Schizosaccharomyces pombe and Magnaporthe oryzae. These GO annotations are current with GOC version from Feb 01, 2019. The processed GAF files from which GO annotations were loaded onto FungiDB can be found in the public folder linked here.

New features in FungiDB 43

  • A beta implementation of the JBrowse Genome Browser is now available from gene record pages on any EuPathDB site.
  • FungiDB gene pages now include a Model Organism Orthologs section for easy navigation to the SGD, PomBase and CGD orthologs of your FungiDB gene.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits.
