We are pleased to announce the release of FungiDB 37

New data in FungiDB 37

Genome Sequence and Annotation: these annotated genomes appear for the first time in FungiDB.

  • Aspergillus campestris IBT 28561 – NCBI BioProject PRJNA217206.
  • Aspergillus novofumigatus IBT 16806 – NCBI BioProject PRJNA217212.
  • Aspergillus steynii IBT 23096 – NCBI BioProject PRJNA217208.
  • Candida auris strain B8441 – Lockhart et al.
  • Cryptococcus neoformans var. grubii KN99 – Friedman et al.

Sequence sites, features and motifs

  • Small RNA sequence profiles during sexual development in Neurospora crassa – Endogenous small RNA profiles from N. crassa protoperithecia and perithecia at 2 and 4 days postfertilization. Wang et al. 2015

Transcriptomics

  • Comparative transcriptomes of yeast and hyphal forms in Histoplasma capsulatum G217B – To compare yeast and hyphal transcript expression patterns cultures were grown at 37C for yeast-phase growth and at room temperature (RT) for hyphal phase growth. Total RNA was then isolated from biological duplicates of yeast or hyphal cultures and strand specific cDNA libraries were created from poly(A)-enriched RNA and sequenced using paired-end deep-sequencing. Gilmore et al. 2015
  • Comparative transcriptomes of yeast and hyphal forms in Histoplasma capsulatum G186AR – To compare yeast and hyphal transcript expression patterns cultures were grown at 37C for yeast-phase growth and at room temperature (RT) for hyphal phase growth. Total RNA was then isolated from biological duplicates of yeast or hyphal cultures and strand specific cDNA libraries were created from poly(A)-enriched RNA and sequenced using paired-end deep-sequencing. Gilmore et al. 2015
  • Comparative transcriptomes of yeast and hyphal forms in Histoplasma capsulatum H88 – To compare yeast and hyphal transcript expression patterns cultures were grown at 37C for yeast-phase growth and at room temperature (RT) for hyphal phase growth. Total RNA was then isolated from biological duplicates of yeast or hyphal cultures and strand specific cDNA libraries were created from poly(A)-enriched RNA and sequenced using paired-end deep-sequencing. Gilmore et al. 2015
  • Multidimensional regulation in transcriptome during sexual development in Neurospora crassa – Genome-wide analysis of N. crassa gene expression during sexual development. Transcriptomic sequencing was primed by random and multitargeted primers and accessed across eight time points encompassing the morphological changes and tissue and cellular development of maturing perithecia. Wang et al. 2014, Wang et al. 2012, and Lehr et al. 2014

Phenotype data

  • Neurospora Genome Project Phenotype Image Collection – New images were added to this data set describing the systematic characterisation of colony growth and morphology of Neurospora crassa knockout mutants. Dunlap et al. 2007, Colot et al. 2006, and Turner G.E. 2011

New features in this release

Beta EuPathDB Workspace

  • Announcing the Beta release of EuPathDB Workspace, a private workspace that can interface with EuPathDB Galaxy and allows for viewing and interrogating private data against data that is already integrated into EuPathDB (public data). The first EuPathDB workspace tool, 'Bigwig files to EuPathDB' exports bigWig files from EuPathDB Galaxy to EuPathDB. The tool creates a EuPathDB Data Set which is managed from the My Data Sets page along with your other private data sets. Each data set also receives a record page which lists all files that the data set contains, offers file management functions and ports files to the genome browser for viewing.

    Video tutorial: Export bigWig files to EuPathDB

Pathway Improvements

  • Semantic zooming of Pathway drawings on Pathway record pages - At low zoom levels, pathway nodes appear as glyphs. At mid zoom levels, the main compound nodes appear as molecular formulas. At high zoom levels, enzymes and side nodes appear as formulas, terms or Enzyme commission numbers. Clicking on a node will retrieve details about the node, regardless of zoom level.
