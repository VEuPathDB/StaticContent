Transcriptomics

  • Candida dubliniensis
    • Transcriptional responses to macrophage phagocytosis — The transcriptional responses to phagocytosis was analysed by incubating fungal cells in mammalian medium in the presence or absence of primary macrophages for 1 hour, followed by RNA extraction and sequencing. Poutain al. 2021. This FungiDB data set has associated data sets.
  • Clavispora lusitaniae
    • Transcriptional responses to macrophage phagocytosis — The transcriptional responses to phagocytosis was analysed by incubating fungal cells in mammalian medium in the presence or absence of primary macrophages for 1 hour, followed by RNA extraction and sequencing. Poutain al. 2021. This FungiDB data set has associated data sets.
  • Lodderomyces elongisporus
    • Transcriptional responses to macrophage phagocytosis — The transcriptional responses to phagocytosis was analysed by incubating fungal cells in mammalian medium in the presence or absence of primary macrophages for 1 hour, followed by RNA extraction and sequencing. Poutain al. 2021. This FungiDB data set has associated data sets.
  • Histoplasma capsulatum
    • Comparative transcriptomes of yeast and hyphal forms in Histoplasma capsulatum G184AR — Analysis of yeast and hyphal transcript expression patterns in Histoplasma capsulatum grown at 37°C and room temperature (RT), respectively. Gilmore al. 2015.
  • Neurospora crassa
    • Gene expression during conidia germination in Neurospora crassa on different media — mRNA were sampled and compared from four time points across conidia germination in Neurospora crassa on different media. Wang al. 2019.
  • Pneumocystis carinii
    • Pneumocystis carinii RNA-Seq — RNA-Seq of three samples each of Peumocystis carinii were isolated from infected rat hosts. Ma al. 2016.

Whole genome sequence of isolates for SNP analysis

  • Aspergillus fumigatus
    • SNP calls on WGS on avian isolates of Aspergillus fumigatus — Isolates taken from aspergillosis-associated avian carcasses, representing 24 bird species from a wide geographic range, and representing individual bird mortalities as well as epizootic events. Lofgren al. 2022.
  • Candida auris
    • SNP calls on WGS of Candida auris isolates obtained by NY Public Health's Wadsworth Center Mycology Laboratory — Whole-genome comparisons of 19 isolates, collected over 72 days. Jacobs et al. 2022.
    • SNP calls on WGS of Candida auris identified in Canada, 2012-2019 — Canadian isolates belonged to the four main genomic clades: Clade I (formerly called South Asian clade, n = 12), Clade II (East Asian, n = 3), Clade III (African, n = 4), and Clade IV (South American, n = 5). Isolates within each clade were clonal. De Luca al. 2022.
    • SNP calls on WGS of Candida auris isolated from apples — Fruits were tested as a source of multidrug-resistant C. auris. Yadav al. 2022.
  • Cryptococcus gattii
    • SNP calls on WGS of C. gattii from North American Pacific Northwest — Cryptococcus gattii in North American Pacific Northwest: whole population genome analysis provides insights into species evolution and dispersal. TGen North.
  • Neurospora discreta
    • SNP calls on WGS of Neurospora discreta crosses — Parental strains FGSC2223 and FGSC4825 were used to generate cross progeny. Waters al. 2020.

New features in {{project}} 63

  • Site search 'Reference Only' organism filter – Large results (> 50,000 hits) from the site search are common since it accesses all {{project}} data/page categories (genes, data types, data sets, organisms, etc.). The new 'Reference Only' toggle offers a one-click filter to reduce the result from all organisms to include only results from VEuPathDB reference orgnanisms.
  • Search details and data sets – Details for the search and the data sets accessed by the search are now available in tabbed pages from the top of the search pages. Previous versions of {{project}} presented this information at the bottom of the page where it was easily missed.
  • Scroll and zoom on gene page browser frames – The dynamic browser views on gene pages now provide active scroll and zoom functions with a reset button to return to the default view. Dynamic browser views are available for Synteny, Alignments, SNPs, Transcriptomics Coverage, Protein Features and Proteomics.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
