2023 VEuPathDB Annual Workshop is In-Person

Join us at the The University of Georgia for the 16th Annual VEuPathDB workshop on May 15 – 19th, 2023. During the workshop, short lectures will precede hands-on exercises where discussion and conversation are encouraged. The objective is for attendees to leave with an understanding of the breadth of data available, how to effectively use VEuPathDB resources to mine the available data, and to use preconfigured workflows to analyze unintegrated data. Attendance is limited to 30 people. Registration, housing, most meals are free during the workshop. Attendees are expected to pay for travel and some dinners. Apply by 3 February 2023.

VEuPathDB is a Dataworks! Prize Finalist

VEuPathDB has been selected as a finalist for the Dataworks! Prize, an NIH/FASEB initiative designed to recognize teams that have excelled in data sharing and reuse. View the DataWorks! page to learn more about the finalists and to vote for us!

VEuPathDB chosen as a Global Core Biodata Resource

VEuPathDB has been selected as a Global Core Biodata Resource (GCBR) which includes 37 resources whose long term funding and sustainability are critical to life science and biomedical research worldwide.

    • The Cryptococcal central nervous system transcriptome during human disease — RNA-Seq profiles of Cryptococcus isolated from human CSF and in comparison with to the same isolate response to the stresses of artificial CSF and within the rabbit subarachnoid space as well as growth in nutritious media with little or no stress Yu et al. 2021.

Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
