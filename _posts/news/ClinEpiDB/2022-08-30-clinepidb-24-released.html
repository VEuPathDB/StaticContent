We are pleased to announce the release of ClinEpiDB 24!

New Features

  • Now you can upload your own data and explore it with all the tools ClinEpiDB has to offer! To get started, go to the Workspace menu in the header and click My User Studies. Then go to the New Upload tab and add your information and file! At the moment, this feature requires that data be in a single comma or tab-delimited text file (.csv, .tsv, or .txt). Once the data are uploaded and installed, which can take up to 2 hours depending on the size of your file, you will be able to access your dataset via the Studies menu and explore and visualize your data just like with any other dataset. Note: Data will only be visible to you unless you choose to share it with another ClinEpiDB user. You can also delete a dataset at any time. Learn more about this new feature on the help page.

New Data

  • The PROMOTE Birth Cohort 3 Randomized Controlled Trial enrolled 782 pregnant women in Uganda and followed them and their infants for 1 year after delivery. The study aimed to compare the risk of adverse birth outcomes and incidence of malaria among HIV-uninfected pregnant women randomized to receive IPTp with monthly sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine (SP) vs dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (DP). The dataset contains data on pregnancy, labor, and delivery, clinical data on mothers and infants, treatment information, and lab test results.
  • The Jilinde Prospective Cohort enrolled 1,135 participants in Kenya and followed them over 3 years. The study aimed to evaluate delivery, uptake, and continuation of oral Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP) by key target groups (men who have sex with men [MSM] and female sex workers [FSW]), adolescent girls and young women (AGYW), individuals in HIV sero-discordant relationships and other high-risk individuals in Kenya.
