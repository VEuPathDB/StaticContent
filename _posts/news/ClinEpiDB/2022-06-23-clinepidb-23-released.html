We are pleased to announce the release of ClinEpiDB 23!

New Features

  • We've made it easier than ever to find studies and variables of interest! No matter where you are on ClinEpiDB, you can use the search bar in the header to search for a term of interest. This search will return Studies that contain the term in their name or description, Variables that contain the term in their variable name, definition, or other metadata, and Variable values that contain the term as part of the value label for a variable. Each result contains links that take you directly to the study, variable, or value of interest! For example, a search for Variables that include "Giardia TAC" returns a number of variables, and the top hit is a variable, Giardia, by TAC result, that is present in 3 different studies, GEMS1, MAL-ED, and PROVIDE.
  • The map visualization tool released two months ago now supports continuous variables — such as age and infection intensity — in addition to categorical variables. For instance, see how ~75% of those enrolled in the SCORE Seasonal S. haematobium Cluster Randomized Trial were children under the age of 14 across sites. Switch between pie plot and bar chart views of the data based on your preference.
  • We have improved the experience of downloading a fully customizable subset of data. The new modal allows you to easily create a table with columns for specific variables you are interested in. Once your table is configured, you can preview and download it.

New Data

  • The Preventing Congenital Syphilis Cluster Randomized Trial was conducted in over 54,000 women in Kinshasa, the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Lusaka, Zambia at 26 antenatal care clinics. The study aimed to determine if behavioral interventions, randomized at the clinic level, improved screening and treatment of syphilis at the first antenatal care visit. The dataset contains variables on obstetric history and Syphilis treatments.
