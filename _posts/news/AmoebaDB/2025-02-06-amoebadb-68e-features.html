New Features

GENE PAGES

We have made improvements to the appearance and navigation of gene record pages:

  • Gene summary at the top of the page is now left-aligned for clarity.
  • Contents menu on the left of the page: Click on a category to expand and reveal subcategories. Selected categories are shaded for enhanced visibility.
  • Central navigation pane: Subcategories are now shaded in grey, with visual indicators to show expandable sections.
  • Font size and padding have been adjusted for better readability.
ORTHOMCL 7.0

A major update of our popular OrthoMCL resource is now available as a beta release, OrthoMCL 7. It is packed with new features, including:

  • OrthoFinder clustering.
  • Similar Groups.
  • Customizable Phylogenetic Trees!
  • New BLAST-based protein mapping tool.

Read more about the new features, and explore the full tutorial.

MY DATA SETS

New data types supported in "My Data Sets", your private data integration component of VEuPathDB. Your BigWig and normalized RNA-Seq data can now be directly uploaded and integrated. See this page for help with this feature and try it for yourself here.

New Content

UPDATED FAQ

As always we strive to be fully transparent about all aspects of VEuPathDB operations, activities and plans. Towards that end, we have updated our frequently asked questions (FAQ) page with questions related to funding status and prospects for the future, VEuPathDB operations, and more.

Outreach Highlights

Community meetings

Towards the end of 2024, we hosted three community forums for an open discussion of our project status and plans, and to answer questions from the research community. If you missed these meetings, recordings are available here:

  • VectorBase community meeting.
  • FungiDB community meeting.
  • Parasitology community meeting.
Virtual workshop

VEuPathDB hosted a free virtual workshop from Dec 11-13, 2024. It was our biggest workshop to date with engaged global participation (over 500 applicants from 63 countries). All materials from the workshop, including recorded lectures and exercises, are publicly available for asynchronous learning.
