VEuPathDB Sustainability

SUBSCRIPTION UPDATE

VEuPathDB currently has over 23,000 registered users, and >650 groups have subscribed to date (both individual labs and multi-group institutional subscriptions, including some that choose to remain anonymous). We are immensely grateful to all subscribers & donors, who make it possible to keep VEuPathDB accessible to all (see the growing list of 2025 subscribers).

HOW YOU CAN HELP

The VEuPathDB subscriptions page now includes additional detail for diverse user groups, including academic labs, industry/biotech, and instructors. Here’s how YOU can help:

  • All VEuPathDB users: Please encourage your PI or team lead to subscribe.
  • Lab and industry heads: Fill out an invoice and subscribe today.
  • Underfunded groups, including LMIC researchers, early-career investigators without grant support, and instructors: Please be sure to complete a subscription form, even if you are unable to afford your fair share of subscription costs this year!
  • Instructors: Tell us about your classroom use of VEuPathDB by completing this brief survey.

BRIDGE FUNDING

We’re pleased to announce that VEuPathDB has received a Discretionary Award from the Wellcome Trust, entitled: “Pioneering a User-Supported Financial Sustainability Model for Essential Database Resources,” providing:

  • Bridge funding as we transition to financially sustainable subscription-based operations, establishing a model potentially applicable to other high-value, cost-effective informatics resources.
  • Subsidies covering subscriptions for underfunded groups, including LMIC researchers, early-career investigators, and instructors.

Note that this award will not fully fund VEuPathDB, but is intended to bridge the gap over the next 1-2 years, as users revise their budgets to cover data access and data-sharing services. >We still need those who have not yet done so to subscribe for 2025, and everyone to budget for 2026 and beyond!

Behind the scenes at VEuPathDB

We’re hard at work reengineering our database architecture to deliver faster pipelines, smoother workflows, and quicker data loading. These improvements lay the foundation for restarting regular data releases in early 2026, and implementation of additional AI tools for improved curation, data-summarization, and data-mining!
