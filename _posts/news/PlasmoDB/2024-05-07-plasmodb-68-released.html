New data in {{project}} 68

Transcriptomics

  • Cytoadherent and wild-type Plasmodium knowlesi (experiment 1) – RNA Seq transcriptome analysis of Plasmodium knowlesi lines with increased iRBC cytoadhesion activity including 4-, 8-, and 24-hr samples of Pan 0, 6, 8 and Pan 13 from Expt 2. Chuang et al. 2022.
  • Cytoadherent and wild-type Plasmodium knowlesi (experiment 2) – RNA Seq transcriptome analysis of Plasmodium knowlesi lines with increased iRBC cytoadhesion activity including 4-, 8-, and 24-hr samples of Pan 0 and Pan 13. Chuang et al. 2022.

Phenotypes

We are excited to provide access to two high throughput phenotypic datasets in Plasmodium knowlesi erythrocytic stages based on piggyBac mutagenesis screens from the Adams and Durrasingh labs. These data are provided in advance of publication to enable early access by the research community:
  • Plasmodium knowlesi H essential genes via piggyBac screen – Plasmodium knowlesi essential genome determined by high density transposon insertion mutagenesis. Zarringhalam et al. prepublication. This data includes associated genome browser tracks for insertion and TTAA sites.
  • Plasmodium knowlesi A1H1 essential genes via piggyBac screen – Plasmodium knowlesi A1H1 essential genome determined by supersaturation transposon insertion mutagenesis. Adams et al. prepublication. This data includes associated genome browser tracks for Plasmodium knowlesiinsertion sites.

In addition to querying the data individually, the tools in PlasmoDB enable cross study comparisons as with this search strategy that compares results from the above two studies and the previously loaded P. falciparum piggyBac study.

Study Metadata and WGCNA Analysis

This data is available in the study explorer. See 'New Features' section below for more details.
  • Dual transcriptomes of malaria-infected Gambian children (Study Explorer) – Clinical and WGCNA analysis variables for the dual transcriptomic (human and parasite) analysis of blood of 46 Gambian children diagnosed with severe or uncomplicated malaria. Lee et al. 2018.

New features in {{project}} 68

  • Long gene product descriptions are now trucated in relation to the width of your browser window. The full product description is available on mouseover and individual transcript product descriptions are available in the All Product Descriptions table on gene pages. Truncated descriptions GIF.
  • The Orthologs and Paralogs table on {{project}} gene pages also serves as a Clustal Omega tool for retrieving alignments between orthologs. This table now includes 'Selection Count' and 'Selection Clear' options for improved tool function. Clustal Omega Counts GIF.
  • The Send To tool is now offered in VEuPathDB.org Strategies. Results created in VEuPathDB.org Strategies can now be ported to {{project}} My Strategies for further investigation. Only genes present in {{project}} species will be included in the ported strategy results. VEuPathDB.org Send To GIF.
  • Newly sorted strategy result tables will now default to page 1 – Search result tables can be sorted based on any column of data. When a table is sorted while viewing page 3, users are returned to page 1 to view the sorted data table.
  • Beta release of new RNA-Seq analysis in {{project}} – A weighted gene co-expression network analysis (WGCNA) was performed on the Dual transcriptomes of malaria-infected Gambian children (RNA-Seq) data set. The analysis produced WGCNA modules of co-expressed genes for the parasite- and host-aligned expression data. WGCNA analysis results are available as:
    • a WGCNA search that returns genes in each WGCNA module.
    • a Study Explorer which offers metadata filters and visualization tools to help conceptualize modules between host and parasite in relation to study variables so that you may discover and choose a module of interest.
