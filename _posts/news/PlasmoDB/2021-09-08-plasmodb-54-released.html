New data in {{project}} 54

Transcriptomics

  • Plasmodium berghei
    • Dietary restriction of P berghei WT and Δkin parasites – RNA-seq transcriptome of synchronized P. berghei wild-type and Δkin parasites collected from mice with normal diet or dietary restriction Mancio-Silva et al. 2017.
  • Plasmodium vivax
    • Patient isolates cultured through the intraerythrocytic development cycle – Plasmodium vivax transcriptional profiling of low input cryopreserved patient isolates through the intraerythrocytic development cycle. Rangel et al. 2020.

Proteomics

  • PfCRK4 regulated proteome at 29 and 37 hpi (quantitative) – Proteome determined at 29- and 37- hour post infection in Plasmodium falciparum using a clone in which PfCRK4 was conditionally destabilized. (+Shield = stable PfCRK4, -Shield = destabilized PfCRK4) Ganter et al. 2017.

Updated Functional Annotation

li>The GeneDB project was taken off-line effective from August 1, 2021. TriTrypDB will now serve as the only source for GeneDB-hosted Kinetoplastida genomes and will continue to provide updated annotations for these curated genomes. However, curatorial staff at VEuPathDB still use Sanger’s backend curation database and will provide updated annotation files in the Sanger FTP site on a monthly basis.

New features in {{project}} 54

  • Genome data set records ({{project}} genome data sets) are updated to increase clarity and reduce redundancy of information on the page. For example, a new table "Explore other Categories of Data Associated with this Genome" contains links to all functional data sets for the organism. The new "Data Context" section provides counts for genes, assembled sequences and transcripts. Functional data set records have received similar updates.
  • The site search, available in the header, is updated to find versions of an ID (e.g. ID.1, ID.2). Specifically, searching for the ID will retrieve ID, ID.1 and ID.2, etc, while searching for ID.1 will retrieve only ID.1
  • The community annotation software, Apollo, was updated to version 2.6.5 (About Apollo).
  • The BLAT sequence search tool is integrated into Apollo. Use the 'Search' tab from the annotator panel to find your sequence of interest in our Apollo-available genomes. (ApolloBLAT.png)


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
