New data in {{project}} 53

Genome Sequence and Annotation

The following chromosome level assemblies with genome annotation appear for the first time in {{project}} 53 and are described in Ramaprasad et al. 2021.

  • Plasmodium vinckei Cameroon EL
  • Plasmodium vinckei brucechwatti DA
  • Plasmodium vinckei lentum DE
  • Plasmodium vinckei petteri CR 2020
  • Plasmodium vinckei vinckei CY

Also newly available in {{project}} 53

  • Plasmodium knowlesi strain A1H1 – The Plasmodium knowlesi A1H1 line has been adapted to continuous culture in human erythrocytes. Benavente et al. 2018.

Transcriptomics

  • Plasmodium berghei
    • Transcriptomes of viable ApiAP2 mutants by directional RNA-seq – Three independent replicates of each strain + WT control were prepared for schizonts (all strains), as well as gametocytes and ookinetes (ap2-o, ap2-o2, ap2-o3 and wt control). Modrzynska et al. 2017.
  • Plasmodium chabaudi
    • In vivo transcriptome in mouse – Samples collected by micro-sampling and terminal artery bleed. Ramaprasad et al. 2019. This data has accompanying data in HostDB (In vivo mouse transcriptome during P. chaubadi infection)
  • Plasmodium falciparum
    • Oocyst and salivary gland sporozoite transcriptome comparison in P. falciparum – RNA sequence analysis of Plasmodium falciparum salivary gland sporozoites and oocyst sporozoites. Lindner et al. 2019. This data has accompanying proteomics data in PlasmoDB (Oocyst sporozoite global proteome in P. falciparum)
    • Intraerythrocytic development cycle transcriptome by DAFT-Seq (3D7, HB3, IT, 2020) – Transcriptome throughout the intraerythrocytic development cycle. RNA sequence analysis of 7 time points (0, 8, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48 hrs) post infection of tightly synchronized Plasmodium falciparum 3D7, HB3 and IT parasites. Chappell et al. 2020.
    • Transcriptome of sequestration phenotypes – RNA sequence analysis of ring-stage parasites from five strains of Plasmodium falciparum with different sequestration phenotypes. Kamaliddin et al. 2021.
  • Plasmodium vinckei
    • Intraerythrocytic development cycle for P. vinckei CY – RNA sequence analysis of time course during the intraerythrocytic cycle at 6, 12, 18, and 24 hours. Ramaprasad et al. 2019. This data has accompanying data in HostDB (Mouse transcriptome during P. vinckei CY infection time course

Proteomics

  • Oocyst sporozoite global proteome in P. falciparum – Proteomic analysis of Plasmodium falciparum oocyst sporozoites. Lindner et al. 2019. This data has accompanying RNA Seq data in PlasmoDB (Oocyst and salivary gland sporozoite transcriptome comparison in P. falciparum)
  • Oocyst sporozoite global proteome in P. yoelii – Proteomic analysis of Plasmodium yoelii oocyst sporozoites. Lindner et al. 2019. This data has accompanying RNA Seq data in PlasmoDB (Oocyst and salivary gland sporozoite transcriptome comparison in P. yoelii)
  • Sporozoite proteome in P. berghei – Proteome of Plasmodium berghei ANKA sporozoites using the highly sensitive timsTOF PRO mass spectrometer. This study used three different methods of sporozoite purification to obtain a comprehensive proteome. Hamada et al. 2019.

Whole genome sequence of isolates for SNPs and CNV

  • Plasmodium chabaudi resequenced strain – Whole genome resequencing data of Plasmodium chabaudi esekanensis, line EF, isolated in Cameroon. Data were aligned to the reference genome and used to call single nucleotide polymorphisms. Ramaprasad et al. 2019.
  • Plasmodium vinckei resequenced strains – Whole genome resequencing data of ten isolates belonging to the five subspecies of Plasmodium vinckei. These include P. vinckei vinckei (CY), P. vinckei brucechwatti (DA and DB), P. vinckei petteri (CR and BS), P. vinckei lentum (DE and DS) and P. vinckei baforti (EH, EL and EE). Ramaprasad et al. 2019.
  • WGS of Plasmodium vivax on the China-Myanmar border – Whole genome sequencing of 23 P. vivax clinical isolates collected on the China-Myanmar border Brashear et al. 2020.
  • Plasmodium yoelii resequenced strains – Whole genome resequencing data of six isolates belonging to four subspecies of P. yoelii. These include P. yoelii yoelii (33X, Ar and CN), P. yoelii nigeriensis (D), P. yoelii killicki (DG) and P. yoelii cameronensis (EL). Ramaprasad et al. 2019..

New features in {{project}} 53

  • Genome data set records ({{project}} genome data sets) are updated to increase clarity and reduce redundancy of information on the page. For example, a new table "Explore other Categories of Data Associated with this Genome" contains links to all functional data sets for the organism. The new "Data Context" section provides counts for genes, assembled sequences and transcripts. Functional data set records have received similar updates.
  • ID searches such as the "site search" or "Identify Genes by ID" have been updated to find versions of an ID (e.g. ID.1, ID.2). Specifically, searching for the ID will retrieve ID, ID.1 and ID.2, etc, while searching for ID.1 will retrieve only ID.1
  • The community annotation software, Apollo, was updated to version 2.6.5 (About Apollo.
  • The BLAT sequence search tool is integrated into Apollo. Use the 'Search' tab from the annotator panel to find your sequence of interest in our Apollo-available genomes. (ApolloBLAT.png)


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
