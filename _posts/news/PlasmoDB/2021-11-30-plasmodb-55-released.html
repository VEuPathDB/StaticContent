New data in {{project}} 55

Genome Sequence

  • Plasmodium vivax PvSY56 – Draft genome of the chimpanzee Pan troglodytes ellioti infective P. vivax strain PvSY56 Loy et al. 2018.

Genome Sequence and Annotation

  • Plasmodium falciparum NF135.C10 – isolated in 1993 from venous blood of a human in Cambodia. The genome was sequenced with PacBio and Illumina methods and assembled using Canu v.1.3 Moser et al. 2020.
  • Plasmodium falciparum NF135.C10 – isolated in 1993 from venous blood of a human in Cambodia. The genome was sequenced with PacBio and Illumina methods and assembled using Canu v.1.3 Moser et al. 2020.

Transcriptomics

  • Plasmodium berghei
    • Dietary restriction of P berghei WT and Δkin parasites – RNA-seq transcriptome of synchronized P. berghei wild-type and Δkin parasites collected from mice with normal diet or dietary restriction Mancio-Silva et al. 2017.
  • Plasmodium vivax
    • Patient isolates cultured through the intraerythrocytic development cycle – Plasmodium vivax transcriptional profiling of low input cryopreserved patient isolates through the intraerythrocytic development cycle. Rangel et al. 2020.

Proteomics

  • PfCRK4 regulated proteome at 29 and 37 hpi (quantitative) – Proteome determined at 29- and 37- hour post infection in Plasmodium falciparum using a clone in which PfCRK4 was conditionally destabilized. (+Shield = stable PfCRK4, -Shield = destabilized PfCRK4) Ganter et al. 2017.

Updated Functional Annotation

  • The GeneDB project was taken off-line effective from August 1, 2021. PlasmoDB will now serve as the only source for GeneDB-hosted Plasmodium genomes and will continue to provide updated annotations for these curated genomes. However, curatorial staff at VEuPathDB still use Sanger’s backend curation database and will provide updated annotation files in the Sanger FTP site on a monthly basis.

Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
