New data in {{project}} 62

Genome sequence and annotation

  • Trypanosoma cruzi Berenice – Sequence was generated using a combination of Illumina short reads and MinION long reads and a hybrid assembly was produced de novo. Díaz-Viraqué et al. 2019 and GCA_013358655.1.

Proteomics

  • Adipose tissue vs bloodstream form proteomes – Proteomics comparison of adipose tissue and bloodstream forms of T brucei Lister 427. Quantification of protein synthesis employed L-Homopropargylglycine methods. Trindade et al. 2022.
  • Proteome during T brucei differentiation – Differential proteomics of T brucei differentiation with the in vitro system based on the inducible expression of RBP6. Doleželová et al. 2020.
  • Proteomic survey of the Leishmania major – High coverage proteome of Leishmania major Friedlin promastigotes Polanco et al. 2022.
  • Quantitative proteomic survey of the Leishmania major Friedlin and null mutants of LPG2 or FUT1. – High coverage proteomes of Leishmania major WT, Δlpg2- and Δfut1s. Polanco et al. 2022.

RNA Sequence

  • Metacyclic vs Procyclic form transcriptomes (2020) – RNA sequence transcriptomes of Trypanosoma brucei metacyclics obtained using an in vitro system compared to the non-infectious procyclic trypanosomes. Doleželová et al. 2020.

New features in {{project}} 62

  • Site search 'Reference Only' organism Filter – Large results (> 50,000 hits) from the site search are common since it accesses all {{project}} data/page categories (genes, data types, data sets, organisms, etc.). The new 'Reference Only' toggle offers a one-click filter to reduce the result from all organisms to include only results from VEuPathDB reference orgnanisms.
  • Search details and data sets – Details for the search and the data sets accessed by the search are now available in tabbed pages from the top of the search pages. Previous versions of {{project}} presented this information at the bottom of the page where it was easily missed.
  • Scroll and zoom on gene page browser frames – The dynamic browser views on gene pages now provide active scroll and zoom functions with a reset button to return to the default view. Dynamic browser views are available for Synteny, Alignments, SNPs, Transcriptomics Coverage, Protein Features and Proteomics.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data September slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
