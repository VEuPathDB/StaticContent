New data in {{project}} 55

Organism Reclassification

Leishmania martiniquensis LEM2494 – This organism previously appeared in TriTrypDB as unclassified Leishmania LEM2494. Warren et al 2021.

Genome Sequence and Annotation

The following genomes appear for the first time in TriTrypDB. They are described in the research article Almutairi et al. 2021, Sci Data with genome announcements referenced below.
  • Leishmania martiniquensis MHOM/TH/2012/LSCM1 – Isolated in 2012 from Thailand. Almutairi et al. 2021, Microbiol Resour Announc.
  • Leishmania orientalis MHOM/TH/2014/LSCM4 – Isolated in 2014 from Thailand. Almutairi et al. 2021, Microbiol Resour Announc.
  • Leishmania enriettii MCAV/BR/2001/CUR178 – Isolated in 2001 from Brazil. Almutairi et al. 2021, Microbiol Resour Announc.
  • Leishmania sp. Ghana 2012 LV757 MHOMGH2012GH5 – Isolated in 2012 from Ghana. Almutairi et al. 2021, Microbiol Resour Announc.
  • Leishmania sp. Namibia MPRO/NA/1975/252/LV425 – Isolated in 1975 from Namibia. Almutairi et al. 2021, Microbiol Resour Announc.
  • Porcisia hertigi MCOE/PA/1965/C119 – Isolated in 1965 from Panama. Almutairi et al. 2021, Microbiol Resour Announc.

ChIP-Seq data

  • Hyperacetylated histone H2A in bloodstream and procyclic form – Genomic distribution of hyperacetylated histone H2A C-terminal combinatorial PTMs in the bloodstream and procyclic life-cycle stages of T. brucei. Provided prepublication from the Hugh Patterton research group.

Updated Functional Annotation

  • The GeneDB project was taken off-line effective from August 1, 2021. TriTrypDB will now serve as the only source for GeneDB-hosted Kinetoplastida genomes and will continue to provide updated annotations for these curated genomes. However, curatorial staff at VEuPathDB still use Sanger’s backend curation database and will provide updated annotation files in the Sanger FTP site on a monthly basis.
  • Summary of annotation updates which include products, GO terms, gene names, synonyms, publications, and notes from curators. These functional annotations are current with GeneDB October 2021 GFF versions.

Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data September slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
